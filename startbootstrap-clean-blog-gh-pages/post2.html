The Christian Mythos

How did the Christian Mythos have a hand in developing 21st century musical culture?

Posted by Randall Harris on November 16th, 2022

Christian Myth in Music

For an example of an historical and extremely influential set of myths, look no further than the Christian mythos. The Christian mythos ticks all ten of Doty’s boxes and it could be reasonably argued that such myths may even fall in line with Doty’s definition of a creative myth as well (relative to the time period it was established/reestablished). The Christian mythos, however, does not necessarily need to start with the Christ Myth. It can, instead, reach as far back as the Old Testament. These Old Testament myths had many grand stories detailing the victory of protagonists facing off against much greater and more powerful foes than themselves. These stories inspired people across all walks of life and era, one such group being African American slaves, particularly in the nineteenth century.

The tales of such great power and patience shown by the incarcerated and lower-class (David, Jonah, Moses, Daniel, etc.) were the catalyst that brought about the “spiritual,” songs created by African Americans that centered on Judeo-Christian stories and themes. These themes are further explored in Sandra Jean Graham’s, Spirituals and the Birth of a Black Entertainment Industry, a work that focuses on the rise of spirituals and their cultural relevance. Something Graham notes in her work is the comparability of Old Testament heroes of myth and the “condition of African Americans.” In this way, the spiritual, and its nearest offshoot, gospel, has symbolized a sort of hope for many enslaved people at the time, that one day a right and just God would see them released from the evils of slavery.
