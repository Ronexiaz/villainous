Modern Influences

The Superhero's Journey.

Posted by Randall Harris on November 17th, 2022

What kinds of modern myths influenced the modern rap genre?

Fast forward roughly fifty years to the 1990’s and you will likely be met with the more modern rap genre. During this leap in time, new myths began to evolve in the eye of popular culture, namely, superheroes. Once again, the wide universes of superheroes tick the many boxes required for Doty’s definition of myth; a vast array of stories, graphic imagery, imaginative, etc., but most importantly, the superhero myths were extremely culturally relevant. So culturally relevant, in fact, that many artists at the time would reference various trademarked super-people in extremely popular tracks.

These references usually involve the artist themselves being a part of a group of super-people or being one or near to one themselves. This sort-of extension of the artist is another way in which Doty’s ideas of a “creative myth” wrap themselves around the rap genre. A notable example can be seen in Kevin “Kool Keith” Thornton’s 2016 single “SUPER HERO,” where the artist uses references and metaphors relating to heroes such as the X-Men, Spiderman, and more. These references also help to establish Kool Keith as a “suprahuman” figure, a rapper that is clearly human while still being plainly above the remaining artists. It’s this sense of superiority and domination over a genre which correlates certain artists with their super counterparts or alter-egos.
